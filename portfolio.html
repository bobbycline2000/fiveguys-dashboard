Five Guys Operations Dashboard

A unified, auto-updating operational view for a multi-source restaurant.

The Problem

A typical Five Guys store runs on six different systems — CrunchTime for inventory and labor, Par Brink for sales, ComplianceMate for food safety, Marketforce for secret shops, Outlook for director communications, plus a handful of one-off PDFs and emails. Each tool tells a piece of the story. None of them talk.

What I Built

One URL. Six data sources. A morning glance at how the store is doing. Sales, labor, food cost variance, compliance grades, secret shop history, team huddle notes — refreshed every morning at 8:05 AM ET via a GitHub Actions pipeline. When a section goes stale, I get notified before Bobby does.

Milestone

Schedule Builder

An auto-built weekly labor schedule that learns from the operator.

The Problem

Building the weekly schedule for 20+ employees, balanced against forecasted labor budget, store rhythms, individual availability and skill levels, takes most managers 2–3 hours every Monday. Done badly, labor % bleeds.

What I Built

A Monday 4 AM scheduled task that drafts the next week's schedule from CrunchTime hours and known store rhythms. Each Monday morning Bobby reviews and edits the draft — and those edits are diffed against the proposal, learned, and applied to next week's run. The schedule converges on Bobby's preferences week over week, and the labor-percentage trend is the real metric.

Playwright Scraper Suite

Auth-aware, selector-stable headless browsers for restaurant SaaS.

Most restaurant operations platforms have no public API. Data lives behind a login, in a JavaScript-rendered dashboard, with quirky filter controls and unstable selectors. The choice is to give up or to scrape carefully.

Outlook Graph Pipeline

MSAL token capture and Microsoft Graph integration for inbox-driven automation.

Why It Exists

The fg2065 mailbox is owned by a corporate Azure tenant where Bobby is not the admin. Standard application-permission OAuth flows are unavailable. The morning brief, the daily payout drafts, and the team-notes panel all needed inbox access anyway.

What I Built

A pipeline that harvests an `outlook.office.com`-scoped MSAL access token from a live Outlook web session via the Chrome MCP browser, then calls Microsoft Graph directly for reads (Inbox messages) and writes (HTML send + Drafts folder). No Azure admin required. No service principal. The token refreshes when the user signs in.
